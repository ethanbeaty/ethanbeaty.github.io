HOOD

Though my ancestors sailed the seas
This was not to be my part
So my tale to you do I impart
As a man whose past now clear he sees
 
I made my way through tides of trees
I entered the town I knew quite well
Though I knew not of the coming swell
The hint of fear in that fateful breeze
 
As I stepped unseen I felt their unease
It is he I saw that I now mention
Tortuous tyrant, tender of tension
Sought now by force to collect his fees
 
From the townspeople did he seize
Copious collection of coin unearned
And a few homes were by him burned
To prove power was at his ease
 
When in time to town returned this disease
To the town he announced his ends
He planned a monument to all his sins
One that his image would in time freeze
 
As I watched him then from aeries
I knew this vile act I could not
Allow so to wood I went to plot
Gone would be Goliath of gilded glories

 
Descend would I from green canopies
Silent walked, as a whisper in the wood
I swiftly moved and donned my hood
And made ready to deliver elegies
 
The first to fall were his deputies
Twang of string afforded arrows flight
Soon would end the town's plangent plight
For they could not escape the bloody melodies
 
 
 
And now times came for he who oversees
The pleas of a "god" I would not heed
There with gasps he drowned in greed
Thus was the last of his lustful liturgies
 
I had then time for obsequies
His cherished change adorned his eyes
So all who saw would then recognize
Those who lived his loathsome litanies
